## 

> **Study Amazon Bedrock first.
> Then study Amazon SageMaker.**

Not because Bedrock is “easier”, but because **this mirrors how GenAI is actually adopted in enterprises**.

---

## Why Bedrock Comes First (Strategic Reasoning)

### 1️ Bedrock Teaches You *What* GenAI Is Used For

Bedrock is where you learn:

* Prompting
* Model behavior
* Latency vs quality trade-offs
* Cost per request
* Guardrails
* Safety
* RAG patterns
* Agent-style workflows
* Real application use cases

This answers:

> *“What business problems does GenAI actually solve?”*

Without this clarity, SageMaker becomes **theory without context**.

---

### 2️ Most Customers Start With Bedrock (Reality Check)

In the real world:

* 70–80% customers **do not train models**
* They want:

  * Chatbots
  * Copilots
  * Document intelligence
  * Internal productivity tools

That is **Bedrock territory**.

If you study SageMaker first, you’ll over-optimize for:

* Training pipelines
* GPUs
* Data science workflows

…which most GenAI customers won’t need on Day 1.

---

### 3️ Bedrock Builds Your Architectural Intuition

With Bedrock you naturally learn:

* Model selection
* Trade-offs between Claude vs LLaMA vs Titan
* Token economics
* Security boundaries
* IAM, VPC integration
* Production deployment patterns

This is **architect-level thinking**, not ML-engineer-only thinking.

---

## When SageMaker Should Come Next (And Why)

Once you understand:

* How GenAI apps behave
* Where they fail
* Where hallucinations occur
* Where accuracy breaks down
* Where RAG isn’t enough

Then SageMaker suddenly makes sense.

SageMaker answers:

> *“How do we go deeper when GenAI becomes strategic?”*

---

### What SageMaker Teaches You (After Bedrock)

* Data preparation
* Feature engineering
* Fine-tuning
* Continued pre-training
* Evaluation
* Experiment tracking
* Governance
* Model versioning
* Enterprise AI maturity

Without Bedrock experience, these feel **abstract**.

With Bedrock experience, they feel **necessary**.
